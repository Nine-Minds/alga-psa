Fixed-fee project billing — UI options

Three integration points into the existing project screen (real header, metadata row, phases panel, kanban, ViewSwitcher) · grounded in ProjectInfo.tsx / ProjectDetail.tsx and the product design system
1

Drawer — the Materials pattern

A "Billing" outline button joins the header actions and opens a right-side drawer with the schedule, budget bar, cost and config. A "Billed: $12,000 of $24,000" bar joins the metadata row. Zero layout change; billing is one click away but never in the way.
2

Band — summary strip above the board

A collapsible strip between the project header and the board: fee/invoiced/ready/margin stats, a horizontal milestone track, and an approve CTA when something is ready. Financial state always visible while working the kanban.
3

View — Kanban | List | Billing

The existing ViewSwitcher gains a third view. Billing takes over the board area as a full workspace (schedule table with allocation footer, budget vs actual, delivery economics) while the phases panel stays for context. Most room to grow (T&M caps, overrides).
Shared scenario: Office 365 Migration for Meridian Health (PRJ-2041) · $24,000 fixed fee, standalone invoices · Deposit 25% (invoiced), Discovery & design 25% (invoiced), Migration cutover 30% (phase completed → ready to bill, waiting 4 days), Final acceptance 20% (manual, pending) · 84.5 h logged at cost, 44% projected margin. All three also add $ milestone badges on phases in the phases panel.
