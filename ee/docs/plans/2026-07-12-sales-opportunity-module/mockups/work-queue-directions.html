Who's a fit?

What each choice commits us to

A — The Docket makes every other screen subordinate: detail, board, and reports are places you visit from the list. Cheapest to build and extend; the brand personality lives in restraint and copy. The EE meeting mode would feel like a different product bolted on.

B — Mission Control makes the working panel the design system: the same deal surface reappears in the board's side sheet, the meeting mode, and QBR packs. Most conventional PSA feel, easiest path for the EE layer, heaviest screen to keep fast.

C — The Advisor makes generated language a first-class UI material — every surface gets a "why" sentence backed by evidence. Most differentiated and most on-thesis; demands a copy system (and eventually the AI seam) good enough to never sound canned.

They share the same skeleton (shell, tabs, chips, evidence vocabulary), so a hybrid is cheap to assemble — e.g. A's list with C's found-money cards, or B's working panel behind A's rows.
