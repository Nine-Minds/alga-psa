What came from where

From A: the single finishable column, the tick affordance, the three-section rhythm (Do today → Going quiet → Money found), and the explicit bottom — "That's everything." The screen stays a short list you can complete, never a dashboard you owe.

From C: the greeting with the found-money total, the why-sentence on every row (evidence speaking in plain language, with the one bold clause that matters), dollar-forward cards for the generators, and the closing lesson drawn from the tenant's own numbers.

The rule that makes it work: one primary button per screen — the most urgent item gets it; everything else is soft or ghost. Why-sentences are assembled from evidence facts (day counts, quote refs, checkpoint states), so they stay true as data changes; the AI seam later upgrades their fluency, not their honesty.
